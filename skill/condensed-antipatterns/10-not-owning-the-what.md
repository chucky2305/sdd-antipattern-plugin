## AP-10: Not owning the "what"

**What it means:** If the whats in the spec (the problem, users, boundaries, tradeoffs) came from an AI draft you rubber-stamped or a template you copied, you inherited someone else's decisions and probably can't defend them. Deciding the whats is where you discover what you don't know. An AI can help with wording; it can't own the intent.

**What a good spec includes (evidence the author owns the "what"):**
- Specific, concrete decisions that only someone who understands the product could make
- Idiosyncratic constraints or tradeoffs that wouldn't appear in a generic template
- Whats the author could defend without saying "the AI suggested it"
